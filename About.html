WHY ART, DESIGN, AND TECHNOLOGY?
My interest in Human- Computer Interaction comes from my interest in people. Through my studies into people and their experiences with visual notions, I realized people's understandings change how they witness the world. My former work has shown me how technology and user experience can motivate even the most mortal happenings maintaining associations to expressing our every accomplishment and misery and linking on a human level. What captivates me most about people is the cyclical association we've with designing these technologies we shape them, and they shape us.

AS A MAKER MIND, I have always leaned into gests and chances with one familiar thread understanding the human experience. I advanced from a child who could purely understand her own experience to a designer who craves to conclude different ones. I was agonized with a constant longing to know why and how. I believe that there's a real association between humans and design. I believe that great design provokes study, expands the imagination, and changes how we move through the humankind.

AS ACROSS-DISCIPLINARY DESIGNER, I'm diligently researching the ways data and innovativeness intersect and drive one another through effective technological design. I prosper in the cross-section of strategy, experience, and tangibility. I believe that successful, transformative results affect from deep collaborations concentrated on elevating users' desires while admitting contextual and social constraints. The space between exploration and design allows me to convert ambiguity into perceptivity to drive conceptualization of new products, map organizational service structures, and design sector- shifting strategies.
